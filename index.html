Scope & philosophy

What this is – A decision-grade capital allocation model for evaluating EV vs ICE ownership cost, charging strategy, and basic solar interaction over a 5–10 year horizon. Built for analytical homeowners who prefer transparent assumptions and sensitivity over black-box answers.

What this is not – Not a live data service, not an hourly dispatch optimiser, not a tax/incentive engine, and not a guarantee of savings. It’s a structured framework to test “what must be true”.

What you get in v1

  • ✓ Cashflow-based TCO (explicit horizon, inputs, and assumptions).
  • ✓ Break-even + NPV, plus “what would need to be true” sensitivity.
  • ✓ A small scenario set (baseline + stress case) to avoid overfitting.
  • ✓ Shareable outputs with assumptions attached + short documentation.

Limitations (by design)

Known limitations in v1
  • Solar interaction is simplified (annual energy basis), not hourly dispatch.
  • Time-of-use tariffs use user-defined effective rates, not half-hourly modelling.
  • Public charging is represented via % mix and unit prices, not session simulation.
  • Residual value uses user assumptions (with sensitivity), not a live depreciation database.
  • Taxes, incentives, grants, and country regulations are not automated.
Will not include (by design)
  • Vehicle databases for every model/trim.
  • Real-time price scraping, APIs, or “live” dashboards.
  • Forecasting claims presented as certainty.
  • Marketing claims of guaranteed savings.
Reason: these features add maintenance overhead and false precision without materially improving decision robustness for most households.
